The most interesting problems tend to live in the spaces between established disciplines. Building accessible developer tools isn't purely a software engineering problem, nor purely a UX problem, nor purely an ethics problem. It's all of these at once, and something more.

## The Builder's Dilemma

There's a tension in modern engineering: specialization is rewarded, but the most important problems often resist specialization. You can spend a career becoming an expert in one narrow slice of reality, or you can try to see how the slices fit together—but rarely both.

I've chosen the latter path, not out of inability to specialize, but out of a conviction that *integration* is itself a form of expertise. Someone needs to speak the languages of multiple domains well enough to build things that bridge them.

## What I'm Building

This site is a home for that work. Here you'll find:

- **Projects** that bridge theory and practice—developer tools, mobile apps, and platforms for human connection
- **Writing** that ranges from technical to philosophical, because the same questions appear in different forms

The goal isn't to be everything to everyone. It's to show that rigorous thinking can drive better engineering, and that the problems worth solving often sit at disciplinary boundaries.
